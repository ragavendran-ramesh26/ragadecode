TNPSC Addendum 8F/2024: 129 New Vacancies Announced in Group II & IIA Examination

Share this article

Published on: 16 Jul 2025

On 14 July 2025, the Tamil Nadu Public Service Commission (TNPSC) released Addendum 8F/2024, an official update to Notification No. 08/2024 concerning the Combined Civil Services Examination – II. This addendum introduces 129 new vacancies across various Group II and IIA posts. Aimed at increasing employment opportunities and addressing emergent administrative needs, this latest revision brings the total vacancy count to approximately 774.

Date: 14 July 2025
Commission: Tamil Nadu Public Service Commission (TNPSC)
Notification No: 08/2024 (Combined Civil Services Examination – II; Group II & IIA)
Addendum No: 8F/2024
Total Additional Vacancies: 129

1. Understanding TNPSC CCSE-II (Group II & IIA)

TNPSC’s Combined Civil Services Examination‑II includes:

  • Group II: Professional-level roles such as Assistant Section Officer, Special Branch Assistant, Assistant Inspector, etc., usually requiring interviews.
  • Group IIA: Clerical roles (non-interview) in departments like Education, Labour, Transport, Forest, Co-operative Societies. Final selection is based purely on performance in written exams.

The original Notification No. 08/2024 (dated 20 June 2024) invited applications for 645 vacancies. Addenda 8A to 8E expanded that number, and Addendum 8F now adds 129 more.

2. Highlights of Addendum 8F/2024

  • New Vacancies Added: 129
  • Major Departments Covered:
    • School Education
    • Co-operative Audit
    • Co-operative Societies
    • Tamil Nadu State Planning Commission

6. Medical & Physical Standards

  • Planning Junior Assistant requires Standard III or better vision.
  • Candidates with defective vision must submit an eye fitness certificate from a government hospital.
  • The post is suitable for all categories under Persons with Benchmark Disabilities.

7. Selection Process

  1. Preliminary Exam – Objective type
  2. Main Exam –
    • Group IIA: MCQ format (no interview)
    • Group II: Descriptive paper and Interview (if applicable)
  3. Final Selection – Based on merit and reservation

8. Strategic Impact for Aspirants

  • Increased vacancies improve chances of selection.
  • Reserved category candidates benefit from additional backlog posts.
  • Removal of interviews in Group IIA ensures transparency.

9. Why Addendums Are Important

  • Helps government fill last-minute vacancies.
  • Adjusts for policy-driven needs like expanding civic departments.
  • Ensures reservation compliance and departmental balance.

10. What Aspirants Should Do Next

  • Review the revised post list and eligibility.
  • Prepare based on updated syllabus (MCQ for Group IIA).
  • Apply through the official TNPSC portal.

Quick Info

What is Addendum 8F/2024 released by TNPSC?
Addendum 8F/2024 is an official update released by the Tamil Nadu Public Service Commission (TNPSC) on 14 July 2025. It introduces 129 additional vacancies to Notification No. 08/2024 for the Combined Civil Services Examination – II, increasing the total vacancy count to approximately 774.
How many new vacancies were added in Addendum 8F/2024?
Addendum 8F/2024 added 129 new vacancies to the TNPSC Combined Civil Services Examination – II.
What is the total number of vacancies after Addendum 8F/2024?
The total number of vacancies after the release of Addendum 8F/2024 is approximately 774, combining the original 645 with additions from addenda 8A to 8F.
Which departments are covered in Addendum 8F/2024?
Addendum 8F/2024 covers the following departments: School Education, Co-operative Audit, Co-operative Societies, and the Tamil Nadu State Planning Commission.
What are Group II and Group IIA posts in TNPSC exams?
Group II posts are professional-level roles that often include an interview, such as Assistant Section Officer or Assistant Inspector. Group IIA posts are non-interview, clerical roles in departments like Education, Labour, and Transport, with final selection based solely on written exams.
What is the newly introduced post in Addendum 8F/2024?
The newly introduced post in Addendum 8F/2024 is Planning Junior Assistant in the Tamil Nadu State Planning Commission, with one vacancy at Pay Level 9.
How many vacancies are now available for the Senior Inspector post?
The revised number of vacancies for the Senior Inspector post in Co-operative Societies is now 411.
What are the revised vacancies for the Assistant post in School Education?
The revised vacancies for the Assistant post in the School Education Department are 171.
What are the educational qualifications required for the Planning Junior Assistant post?
Candidates must possess any degree from a UGC-recognized university to be eligible for the Planning Junior Assistant post.
Is the Planning Junior Assistant post open to persons with disabilities?
Yes, the Planning Junior Assistant post is suitable for all categories under Persons with Benchmark Disabilities, provided candidates meet the required vision standards.
What is the age limit for General Category applicants for the Planning Junior Assistant post?
For General Category applicants, the minimum age is 18 and the maximum is 32 years. However, the maximum is 42 for PwBD, 50 for ex-servicemen, and there is no age limit for destitute widows.
Is there an upper age limit for reserved category candidates?
No, there is no upper age limit for reserved category candidates (BC, MBC, SC, ST).
What are the vision requirements for Planning Junior Assistant?
Candidates must have Standard III or better vision. Those with defective vision must submit an eye fitness certificate from a government hospital.
What is the selection process for TNPSC Group IIA posts?
The selection process involves a preliminary objective exam, a main MCQ exam, and final selection based on merit and reservation, with no interview for Group IIA.
Does the Planning Junior Assistant post require an interview?
No, the Planning Junior Assistant post is under Group IIA, which does not require an interview. Selection is based solely on written exams.

Aspirants should review the updated list of posts and eligibility criteria, adjust their preparation based on the MCQ format for Group IIA, and apply through the TNPSC official portal.

The Planning Junior Assistant post is classified under Pay Level 9.

What does the selection process for Group II posts involve?
The selection process for Group II includes a preliminary objective exam, a descriptive main exam, and an interview, with final selection based on performance and reservation.

Group IIA selection is based purely on written examination scores with no interviews, reducing subjectivity and ensuring a transparent recruitment process.
