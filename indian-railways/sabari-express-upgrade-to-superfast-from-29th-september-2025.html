Sabari Express Upgrade to Superfast (From 29th September 2025)

Share this article
WhatsApp Twitter Facebook
Link copied!
Published on: 25 Jul 2025

Indian Railways has officially announced the conversion of the iconic Sabari Express (17229/30) into a Superfast Express service with new train numbers 20630/20629, effective from September 29, 2025. This upgrade not only reduces travel time between Thiruvananthapuram (TVC) and Secunderabad (SC) but also streamlines operations with enhanced scheduling, better punctuality, and essential operational changes. Below is a complete breakdown of the changes, revised timings, and route benefits.

Summary of Changes

Previous Train Nos.New Train Nos.RouteEffective FromChange
17229 / 1723020630 / 20629Thiruvananthapuram ↔ Secunderabad (TVC–SC–TVC)29th September 2025Converted to Superfast

Why the Change?

Indian Railways has upgraded the long-distance Sabari Express to Superfast status:

  • New Numbers: 20630 (TVC–SC) and 20629 (SC–TVC)
  • Effective: Journey Commencing On (JCO) 29.09.2025
  • Reason: Reduce travel time and improve punctuality
  • Impact: Superfast surcharge applies

Quick Info

What change has been made to the Sabari Express train?
The Sabari Express has been upgraded to Superfast status, resulting in a change in its train numbers and a slight reduction in travel time.
What are the new train numbers for Sabari Express?
The new train numbers are 20630 (Thiruvananthapuram to Secunderabad) and 20629 (Secunderabad to Thiruvananthapuram).
When will the new Superfast Sabari Express service start?
The revised service will be effective for journeys commencing on or after 29th September 2025.
What was the previous train number for Sabari Express?
The previous train numbers were 17229 (TVC–SC) and 17230 (SC–TVC).
Why was Sabari Express upgraded to Superfast?
Indian Railways upgraded it to reduce travel time, improve punctuality, and enhance overall operational efficiency.
Does the upgrade to Superfast affect ticket prices?
Yes, a Superfast surcharge will now apply to tickets for this train.
What is the revised travel time from Thiruvananthapuram to Secunderabad?
The revised duration is approximately 40 hours and 15 minutes, reduced from around 41 hours.
What is the revised travel time from Secunderabad to Thiruvananthapuram?
The new duration is approximately 40 hours and 20 minutes.
What are some key stops on the route from Thiruvananthapuram to Secunderabad?
Key stops include Ernakulam Town, Palakkad Junction, Erode Junction, Katpadi Junction, Gudur, Tenali, and Guntur.
What are the key halts on the return journey from Secunderabad to Thiruvananthapuram?
Important halts include Guntur, Tenali, Gudur, Renigunta, Katpadi, and Palakkad before reaching Thiruvananthapuram.
Are there maintenance halts during the journey?
Yes, there are watering and maintenance stops at stations like Palakkad, Erode, Guntur, and Renigunta.
What operational change improved the punctuality of Sabari Express?
The train was decoupled from Padmavati Express, allowing for improved schedule adherence and timing consistency.
What is the departure time of train 20630 from Thiruvananthapuram?
Train 20630 departs from Thiruvananthapuram Central at 06:45 AM.
What is the arrival time of train 20630 at Secunderabad?
It arrives at Secunderabad Junction at 11:00 AM the next day.
When does train 20629 depart from Secunderabad?
Train 20629 departs from Secunderabad Junction at 14:25 PM.

In-Depth Answers

When does train 20629 reach Thiruvananthapuram?
It arrives at Thiruvananthapuram Central at 18:20 PM the next day.
What corridor does Sabari Express primarily serve?
It serves the long-distance corridor between Kerala (Thiruvananthapuram) and Telangana (Secunderabad), passing through Tamil Nadu and Andhra Pradesh.
Is the train schedule optimized after the upgrade?
Yes, the revised timings have been optimized to improve efficiency and reduce travel delays.
