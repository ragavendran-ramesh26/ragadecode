Published on: 4 Jul 2025

What Is the Gangman Hut?

A Gangman Hut is a resting shelter for railway track maintenance staff, known as "gangmen." These structures are critical in providing workers a space to take breaks and store equipment while working on the tracks. The 3D-printed Gangman Hut at Parvatipuram is the first of its kind in the Indian Railways ecosystem.

Built in Record Time with 3D Concrete Printing

The construction was carried out by Rail Vikas Nigam Limited (RVNL), a public sector undertaking under the Ministry of Railways. Using 3D concrete printing technology, the hut was built in just 25 days‚Äîa fraction of the time traditional construction would take.

The structure spans approximately 100 square meters (1,076 square feet) and was printed using Ultra-High-Performance Concrete (UHPC) and lightweight concrete. The technology allows for:

  • Faster construction with minimal human labor
  • Reduced material waste
  • Greater design flexibility
  • Lower environmental impact

Future of 3D Printing in Railways

This successful implementation opens the door to more 3D-printed structures across the country. From office spaces and control rooms to restrooms and other utility buildings, Indian Railways is set to transform how it builds and maintains infrastructure.
