Bally Station Hero: RPF Officer Saves Man from Suicide Attempt

Published on: 8 Jul 2025

At Bally railway station in West Bengal, a moment of quick thinking turned a possible tragedy into a story of heroism. On July 5, 2025, a man tried to take his own life by jumping in front of an oncoming local train. But thanks to the alertness and courage of Railway Protection Force (RPF) constable HC Surendra Kumar, the man was saved just in time. The officer’s fast action not only saved a life but also highlighted the importance of RPF’s ongoing safety mission. Here's a look at the key facts:

What Happened

  • A man attempted to jump in front of a moving train at Bally station.
  • HC Surendra Kumar, on duty nearby, saw the man and reacted instantly.
  • He ran toward the danger and pulled the man off the tracks moments before the train passed.
  • The entire event was captured on CCTV and later went viral on social media.

Why This Act Matters

  • The rescue was not just brave — it was life-saving.
  • HC Kumar risked his own safety to save a stranger.
  • His actions prevented a major tragedy on the railway platform.

Operation Jeewan Raksha

  • The RPF runs a special mission called Operation Jeewan Raksha.
  • It focuses on preventing suicides and saving lives at railway stations.
  • HC Kumar’s action is a model success under this campaign.

Social Media Recognition

  • RPF India shared the incident on their official Twitter account.
  • The post praised Kumar’s courage and spread awareness about Operation Jeewan Raksha.

A Final Note

  • Suicide is not a solution. Help is always available, even when it doesn’t feel like it.
  • If you or someone you know is struggling, don’t stay silent — talk to a friend, family member, or mental health professional.
  • Just like HC Surendra Kumar stepped in to save a life, there are people out there ready to support you too.
