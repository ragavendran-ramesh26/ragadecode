Indigo Flight Makes Emergency Landing in Chennai After Mid-Air Technical Malfunction

Published on: 29 Jun 2025

A mid-air emergency involving an Indigo Airlines flight from Chennai to Hyderabad sent shockwaves through passengers and airport authorities on Saturday evening. The flight, which had 165 onboard including 159 passengers and 6 crew members, made an emergency landing at Chennai International Airport following a mechanical malfunction while flying over Andhra Pradesh.

The swift response by the pilot and the Chennai Airport authorities ensured the safety of everyone onboard, preventing what could have been a tragic incident.

Routine Flight Turns Risky Over Nellore

The Indigo domestic flight took off at 3:40 PM from Chennai Domestic Terminal and was expected to land in Hyderabad by 5:00 PM. As the aircraft cruised past Nellore in Andhra Pradesh, passengers reported hearing abnormal noises from the engine area. The cockpit then alerted Chennai Air Traffic Control after onboard diagnostics detected a technical fault.

The malfunction, described by insiders as a "sudden engine disturbance", led to a decision to immediately return the aircraft to its point of departure.

Heroic Action by the Pilot and Ground Control

Upon receiving the distress signal, Chennai ATC promptly cleared the flight for an emergency landing. Emergency services, including firefighting and medical units, were mobilized along the designated runway.
