Chennai Metro Phase 2: Tunnel Boring Completed from Panagal Park to Kodambakkam

Published on: 25 Jul 2025

The Chennai Metro Rail Phase 2 project has achieved a major milestone with the successful completion of tunnel boring work between Panagal Park and Kodambakkam. The achievement marks a key progress point in the city’s expanding urban transit infrastructure.

Key Highlights

DetailDescription
ProjectChennai Metro Phase 2
CorridorCorridor 4: Lighthouse to Poonamallee Bypass
Total Length26.8 km
Stations12 underground + 18 elevated
Tunnel Boring SegmentPanagal Park to Kodambakkam
Tunnel Length Completed2.47 km
Tunnel Boring Machine (TBM) UsedNamed “Mayil”
Start Date of Tunnel WorkMay 2, 2024
Completion DateJuly 2025

Tunnel Boring Machine 'Mayil' Hits Target

  • The TBM named “Mayil” (meaning peacock in Tamil) successfully completed the 2.47 km stretch of tunnel from Panagal Park to Kodambakkam.
  • This operation navigated beneath densely built structures and elevated roads, posing several technical challenges.
  • The Chennai Metro Rail Limited (CMRL) team—including officials and engineers—celebrated the breakthrough moment when the TBM surfaced at Kodambakkam.

Corridor Overview: Lighthouse to Poonamallee

  • The stretch is part of Corridor 4 of Phase 2, which spans from Lighthouse in Marina Beach to Poonamallee’s Bypass road, totaling 26.8 km.
  • The corridor includes:
    • 12 underground stations
    • 18 elevated stations
  • This corridor aims to connect densely populated areas with commercial and transit hubs, easing congestion and improving daily commute efficiency.

Smooth Execution Without Public Disruption

  • According to the CMRL, the tunneling was completed without causing disruption to public life or surface traffic, despite the complexity of tunneling beneath critical city infrastructure.
  • The success is attributed to advanced planning, modern tunneling technology, and expert execution.

What’s Next?

  • More tunnel breakthroughs are expected along this corridor in the coming months.
  • Construction of underground stations and integration of systems (tracks, ventilation, power supply) will follow in this segment.
  • Once fully operational, Corridor 4 will significantly enhance East-West connectivity in Chennai.

Conclusion

The completion of the Panagal Park to Kodambakkam tunnel segment marks a major step in Chennai Metro Phase 2’s progress. With modern tunneling machines like “Mayil” and meticulous coordination, Chennai is steadily moving towards a more connected, efficient, and sustainable public transport future.
