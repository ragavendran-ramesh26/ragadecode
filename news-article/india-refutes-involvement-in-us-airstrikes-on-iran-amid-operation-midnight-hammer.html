India Refutes Involvement in US Airstrikes on Iran Amid Operation Midnight Hammer

Published on: 24 Jun 2025

New Delhi, June 2025 — In the wake of intensifying global scrutiny, the Indian government has firmly denied claims that the United States used Indian airspace to launch strikes on Iran. These speculations followed the high-profile Operation Midnight Hammer, a U.S.-led offensive that targeted Iranian nuclear facilities.

Official Denial from India

Multiple online posts and social media threads recently alleged that India facilitated the United States in its air raids by granting access to its airspace. In response, India’s Press Information Bureau (PIB) activated its Fact Check Unit, which swiftly debunked the rumors, labeling them as "fake and misleading."

“There is no truth to the claims that Indian airspace was used in the U.S. bombing campaign,” stated the official release.

The clarification was aimed at calming the public and preventing the spread of misinformation that could potentially impact India's diplomatic standing amid an already fragile geopolitical atmosphere.

The Strike That Shook the Region

The U.S. military action—dubbed Operation Midnight Hammer—involved seven B-2 Spirit stealth bombers originating from Whiteman Air Force Base in Missouri. These aircraft reportedly conducted direct precision strikes on three Iranian nuclear sites, avoiding any unauthorized airspace, including that of India.

U.S. Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff General Dan Kaine presented a comprehensive map and mission timeline to the international media. The data confirmed that none of the B-2 bombers entered Indian airspace, reinforcing India's assertion of non-involvement.

Additionally, another squadron of B-2 bombers reportedly flew over the Pacific Ocean, acting as decoy formations to mislead Iranian radar systems.

India’s Diplomatic Stand

Following the U.S. strikes, Prime Minister Narendra Modi placed an urgent call to Iranian President Peshkan. The 45-minute conversation was aimed at de-escalating tensions and reaffirming India’s commitment to regional peace.

“India urges all parties to exercise restraint and engage in constructive dialogue,” said PM Modi during the call.

He also extended gratitude for the safe return of Indian nationals from Iran, underlining India’s focus on protecting its citizens amid rising hostilities in the Middle East.

Conclusion: India’s Neutral Posture

As the conflict between the U.S. and Iran escalates, India is carefully balancing its diplomatic responsibilities. By disavowing any military involvement, reiterating neutrality, and engaging in peaceful diplomacy, India seeks to play the role of a responsible global stakeholder.

While misinformation spreads rapidly in the digital age, India’s prompt rebuttal and clear communication reassert its non-aligned and sovereign stance in matters of foreign conflict.
