Milestone School Plane Crash: Inside the Uttara Tragedy Involving Bangladesh’s F-7 BGI Fighter Jet

Published on: 22 Jul 2025

A horrifying scene unfolded in the heart of Uttara when a Bangladesh Air Force F-7 BGI fighter jet plummeted into the campus of Milestone School and College, resulting in one of the most devastating civilian tragedies in recent national memory. The milestone plane crash left behind a trail of fire, chaos, and sorrow as students, teachers, and bystanders were caught in the sudden disaster.

What Happened at Milestone College?

Just after 1:00 p.m. local time, an F7 fighter jet Bangladesh Air Force aircraft, specifically a Chengdu F-7 (known locally as F-7 BGI), took off from a military airbase on a routine training mission. Within minutes, the aircraft experienced a critical failure. The pilot, Flight Lieutenant Towkir, made a desperate attempt to avoid densely populated zones in Uttara, ultimately crashing into a school building at Milestone College Uttara.

Eyewitnesses recall a massive explosion followed by flames engulfing parts of the building. Students were attending afternoon classes at the time of the incident, turning the tragedy into a harrowing milestone tragedy that has shocked the nation.

Deaths, Injuries, and Destruction

The uttara plane crash resulted in at least 20 confirmed deaths, including school children, faculty members, and the pilot himself. More than 170 others sustained injuries—many critically. Emergency teams worked for hours to extract survivors from the debris of Uttara Milestone School.

Local hospitals, including burn units and trauma care centers, were overwhelmed as victims poured in. The Milestone School Plane Crash has prompted nationwide mourning and renewed scrutiny of the Bangladesh Air Force's training operations over urban landscapes.

The Aircraft: F-7 BGI and Its History

The aircraft involved was a F7 fighter jet, a modernized variant of the Mig 21 lineage. The F-7 BGI is known for its lightweight design and agility, used extensively by the Bangladesh Air Force for pilot training. This f7 aircraft was part of a fleet acquired in the last decade to bolster air defense capabilities.

The F-7Bgi, though advanced in instrumentation, has been under discussion among military analysts due to its aging airframe and historical safety concerns. The recent Bangladesh Air Force plane crashes have raised alarms regarding the reliability and maintenance of such platforms.

The Pilot: Flight Lieutenant Towkir

Flight Lieutenant Towkir, the young pilot operating the doomed F 7 fighter jet, had recently completed his final supervised sessions and was cleared for his first solo flight. According to ISPR (Inter-Services Public Relations), he attempted to direct the aircraft away from residential and school zones despite a rapidly deteriorating situation.

His selfless act likely saved hundreds of lives, though it cost him his own. His bravery is now hailed across the country, turning him into a national symbol of sacrifice.

Milestone School and College: A Place of Learning Turned to Ashes

Located in the Diabari area of Uttara, Milestone School and College—also known as Milestone College—is a prominent private institution catering to thousands of students across academic levels. The milestone college plane crash has left a permanent scar on the campus and its community.

The Milestone School Uttara campus saw its buildings shattered, classrooms destroyed, and lives lost in seconds. Parents, teachers, and rescue workers rushed in frantically to save children from the rubble. The milestone school plane crash has devastated not only a campus but the spirit of an entire academic family.

Reaction: Mourning Across Bangladesh

Across bd news platforms and social media, the crash has dominated news today, with live updates, tributes, and demands for accountability. Outlets such as Prothom Alo News, Dhaka Tribune, Somoy TV, and even international networks like Al Jazeera covered the story with depth and urgency.

The Bangladesh Air Force, along with government officials, declared a day of national mourning. Flags were flown at half-mast, and special prayers were held in memory of the victims. The milestone college tragedy has become a moment of unified grief in Bangladesh's modern history.

Investigation Underway

The ISPR has confirmed that a high-level inquiry committee has been formed to investigate the plane crash. The committee will examine flight records, black box data, aircraft maintenance logs, and base protocol. Safety measures involving f7 fighter jets and their training routes will also be reassessed to prevent future incidents over civilian areas.

There is a growing call for grounding aging aircraft like the F-7 series and replacing them with safer alternatives.

National Voices and Public Outcry

From grieving parents to national leaders, voices across Bangladesh have expressed anger and anguish. Citizens have questioned why military flight paths still intersect urban zones like Uttara. Hashtags like #MilestoneTragedy and #UttaraPlaneCrash have trended across platforms, reflecting widespread demand for reform.

At the same time, the heroism of Flight Lieutenant Towkir has struck a deep emotional chord. Candlelight vigils and remembrance boards have begun appearing across schools and public spaces.

The Way Forward

This catastrophe must serve as a turning point in civil-military airspace management. The milestone college plane crash exposes urgent gaps in aviation safety, especially concerning training operations near schools and residential zones.

As investigations progress, the people of Bangladesh await not just answers—but action.

Closing Thoughts

The Milestone School plane crash is more than a tragic headline—it's a painful reminder of lives lost in moments that could have been prevented. As Bangladesh grieves, the nation also reflects. This is a time for reform, remembrance, and responsibility.

May the souls lost in this plane crash rest in peace, and may the courage of one pilot inspire future safety for all.
