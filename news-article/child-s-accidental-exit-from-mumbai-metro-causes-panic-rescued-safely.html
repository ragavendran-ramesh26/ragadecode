Child's Accidental Exit from Mumbai Metro Causes Panic: Rescued Safely

Published on: 3 Jul 2025

A heart-stopping incident occurred at the Bandkur Nagar Metro Station in Mumbai, Maharashtra, where a two-year-old child accidentally exited a metro train alone. This unexpected event led to a temporary panic at the station but ended safely due to the quick actions of metro staff.

What Happened?

At Bandkur Nagar Metro Station, when a metro train stopped, its doors opened as usual. During this moment, a two-year-old child who was traveling with family mistakenly stepped out of the train onto the platform without their parents noticing. As soon as the doors closed and the train prepared to depart, the family realized the child was missing.

Rising Panic

The scene quickly became tense. The parents, unaware their child had exited the train, were unable to stop the metro from departing. Station staff and passengers noticed the child alone on the platform, and an alert was raised immediately.

Metro Staff’s Quick Response

A metro employee named Sodangan, who was on duty at the time, swiftly informed the metro train driver about the situation. Acting quickly, the train was halted, and doors were reopened. The child was reunited with their parents without any harm.

Safety Measures in Action

Thanks to the metro staff’s timely reaction and careful coordination, the child was safely handed back to the parents. The child suffered no injuries or trauma. This incident highlighted both the vulnerability of small children in crowded public transport and the importance of alert and trained metro personnel.

Conclusion

While the incident caused brief chaos at the metro station, the child was rescued safely due to the efficient action of the metro staff. This serves as a reminder to always keep a close watch on children in public places and applauds the metro team’s vigilance.
