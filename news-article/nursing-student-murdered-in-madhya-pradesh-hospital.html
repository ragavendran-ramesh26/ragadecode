Nursing Student Murdered in Madhya Pradesh Hospital

Published on: 4 Jul 2025

A horrifying incident has shaken Madhya Pradesh and the nation, as a brutal murder of an 18-year-old nursing student at the Narsinghpur District Hospital was caught on camera. The disturbing footage, now viral on social media, has raised serious questions about safety and public apathy in broad daylight crimes.

Location: Narsinghpur District Hospital, Madhya Pradesh
Victim: Jyoti Ahirwar (18), Nursing Student
Accused: Abhishek Koshti (22), Engineering Dropout

Shocking Crime Caught on Camera

In a shocking turn of events, a young nursing student was killed while on duty at Narsinghpur District Hospital. The accused, Abhishek Koshti, reportedly entered the hospital with a knife and slit the student’s throat in front of staff, patients, and visitors. The crime happened in broad daylight and was captured by a bystander on his mobile phone.

The 47-Second Attack Timeline

  • 2:14 PM: Accused enters hospital with 10" knife (no security check)
  • 2:16 PM: Approaches victim at Nurse Station 3
  • 2:17 PM: Single slash to throat - fatal wound
  • 2:19 PM: Walks away casually as victim collapses

Victim Was on Duty

At the time of the attack, the student was attending to patients. Witnesses report that many people were present — doctors, patients, and their families — yet no one intervened. Despite the crowded hospital, no attempt was made to stop the accused.

Background and Motive

Preliminary investigations suggest the motive might be linked to a one-sided love affair. The victim had allegedly stopped communicating with the accused, which might have enraged him. Police have stated that after the murder, the accused tried to commit suicide but failed and attempted to flee. He was later arrested and is now in police custody.

Public Inaction Raises Concern

What has sparked further outrage is the public's passive response. Even as the crime unfolded, onlookers stood by or recorded videos instead of intervening. Experts and citizens alike have criticised the lack of action that might have saved the girl's life.

Ongoing Investigation

The police are currently investigating the incident to determine the full motive and circumstances. The case has ignited debates around women's safety, public responsibility, and the mental health of young individuals.

Current Case Status

  • Charges Filed:

    • IPC 302 (Murder)
    • IPC 506 (Criminal Intimidation)
    • IPC 354D (Stalking)

  • Public Response:

    • 2.5L signatures for "Jyoti's Law" petition
    • Health Ministry orders audit of all MP hospitals

Conclusion

This tragic incident from a hospital — a place meant for healing — has exposed alarming issues in our society. The silence of the bystanders is as disturbing as the crime itself. Justice for the victim must be swift, and measures need to be taken to ensure such acts are never repeated.
