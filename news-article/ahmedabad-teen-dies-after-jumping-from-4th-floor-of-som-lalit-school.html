Ahmedabad Teen Dies After Jumping From 4th Floor of Som Lalit School

Share this article
WhatsApp Twitter Facebook
Link copied!
Published on: 27 Jul 2025

Ahmedabad was shaken by a tragic incident when a 15‑year‑old Class 10 student from Som Lalit School, located in Navrangpura, leapt from the fourth-floor balcony during recess on Thursday, July 24, 2025. The girl was rushed to hospital but died later that evening from severe injuries.

What Happened?

CCTV footage reviewed by authorities shows the student walking calmly in the fourth-floor corridor while twirling a keychain. Without warning, she climbed over the parapet and jumped. A friend reportedly tried to stop her but was unable to.

Who Was Affected?

The incident involved a Class 10 student whose identity is withheld for privacy. Witnesses included classmates and school staff who responded after the fall.

Where Did It Occur?

  • Location: Som Lalit School, Navrangpura, Ahmedabad
  • Site: Fourth-floor balcony/corridor, outside classroom area
  • Timing: During the afternoon recess break

When Did It Happen?

  • Date: July 24, 2025
  • Time: Approximately 12:27–12:40 PM IST during recess period

Why Is This Significant?

Police said the motive is unclear. No note was found, and initial investigation cites possible mental distress. The school and family report no prior complaints or visible warning signs. Authorities are interviewing those who were present and will look into her recent history and behavior. CCTV evidence is central to the ongoing inquiry.

How Was Emergency Response Handled?

  • The student was taken by 108 Emergency Services to a nearby hospital (Nidhi Hospital) and later transferred to another facility.
  • She sustained head trauma, fractures, and severe concussion. Despite medical efforts, she succumbed to her injuries around 10 PM.
  • Police registered an accidental death report and have secured footage and witness statements. No foul play is presently suspected. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

Final Note

The tragedy has drawn attention to student mental health and the urgent need for supportive environments within educational institutions. The city’s education authorities have announced steps to expand mental health programs under Project Sarthi, assigning trained counselors in schools to address student distress.

For readers seeking responsible coverage, we recommend avoiding speculation and respecting family privacy while awaiting further updates.
