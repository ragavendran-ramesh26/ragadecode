Fake Delivery Man Attacks Woman in Pune

Published on: 6 Jul 2025

Around 7:30 pm on Wednesday, a 22-year-old woman in Pune’s upscale Kondhwa society was allegedly raped by a man posing as a courier delivery agent. The accused reportedly used a chemical spray to render her unconscious, then assaulted her, took a selfie with her phone, and left a chilling message stating, "I will be back."

Incident Summary

  • The man arrived at the victim’s door claiming to deliver a bank envelope.
  • He asked for a pen or OTP for delivery verification.
  • As the woman turned to fetch it, he slipped inside, locked the door, and sprayed a substance on her face. She lost consciousness immediately.
  • Police suspect the spray was used to incapacitate her for nearly an hour.
  • Upon regaining consciousness around 8:30 pm, the woman alerted her relatives, who notified the police.
  • The accused used her phone to take a selfie and left a message threatening to share her photos online.
  • A case has been filed under BNS sections 64 (rape), 77 (voyeurism), and 351(2) (criminal intimidation) at Kondhwa Police Station.

Investigation Underway

  • Pune Police have mobilized 10 teams (crime branch + zonal teams) to trace the suspect.
  • CCTV footage captured the assailant’s face; sketch artists are at work.
  • Forensic experts were deployed to analyze the scene and possible chemical residue.

Public Impact & Safety Concerns

  • The crime has triggered alarm in Pune, especially since it occurred in a society equipped with CCTV and reputed high security.
  • Security policies for verifying delivery personnel are under scrutiny, with many questioning how the accused bypassed safety protocols.

This harrowing case highlights the vulnerabilities women face within supposedly secure urban environments. It underscores urgent needs for stricter visitor verification, improved forensic capabilities at crime scenes, and immediate systemic responses to protect women in their homes.
