Editorial Policy

At RagaDecode.com, we are committed to publishing news and insights that are decoded, verified, and responsibly produced. Our editorial model uniquely blends AI tools, human expertise, and fact-driven research to provide articles that are both clear and credible. This policy outlines the standards, principles, and workflow that guide our journalism.

Our Editorial Philosophy

We believe in:

  • Clarity over clutter
  • Fact over speculation
  • Context over clickbait
  • Transparency over ambiguity

Our aim is to decode:

  • Complex current affairs
  • Regional/geopolitical analysis
  • Industry shifts in technology, automobiles, and finance
  • Cultural insights through travel and social trends

We prioritize reader-first journalism. That means all editorial choices serve truth, context, and accessibility, not popularity or monetization alone.

How We Create Our Articles

1. Topic Selection

Articles are selected based on:

  • Public interest value
  • Trending topics on YouTube/news aggregators
  • Major policy updates or global developments
  • Automotive product launches or financial shifts
  • Reader demand based on search patterns

We do not chase virality for clicks. If a story has no credible basis or offers no meaningful insight, it is not covered.

2. Source Collection

We collect source material from:

  • YouTube news videos (especially Tamil/regional commentary)
  • Official government websites (e.g., PIB, MEA, MoD, ISRO, RBI)
  • International newswires (Reuters, AFP, BBC, Al Jazeera)
  • Company press rooms (Apple, Tata Motors, etc.)
  • Academic research, whitepapers, and policy briefs when needed

Each story is built on multiple cross-verified sources.

3. Transcription & Initial Drafting

For video-based decoding:

  • We extract full transcripts from long-form YouTube discussions
  • We summarize the speech using AI tools like ChatGPT/Gemini (only as a helper)
  • Our editors rewrite the transcript into an article using facts + context

This makes complex oral content readable, referenceable, and factual.

4. AI-Assisted Verification

We use AI tools for:

  • Validating claims made in the transcript
  • Checking for historical accuracy and geopolitical consistency
  • Identifying if the news is fabricated, conspiracy-driven, or misleading

AI is never used to blindly generate content. Every AI-generated summary or insight is reviewed by a human editor for logic, relevance, and consistency.

    • Intro‚ÄìBody‚ÄìConclusion format
    • SEO-optimized metadata and tags
  • We avoid passive language, cluttered writing, and vague claims

Every article is reviewed for:

  • Grammar and structure
  • Source attribution
  • Internal consistency
  • Factual alignment with verified material

Fact-Checking Principles

Our fact-checking follows a 3-tier process:

1. Source Validation

  • Claims must be supported by at least two reputable sources
  • If a story is based on government or legal documents, we link to the original PDFs

2. Public Cross-Reference

  • We use open data tools, Google Fact Check, and archives to confirm:
    • Dates
    • Events
    • Quotes
    • Company/product details
    • Historic timelines

3. Context Evaluation

  • We verify not only what was said, but why, when, and how it matters
  • Sensational or manipulated quotes are avoided unless supported by context
