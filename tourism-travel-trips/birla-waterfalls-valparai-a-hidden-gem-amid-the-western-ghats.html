Birla Waterfalls, Valparai: A Hidden Gem Amid the Western Ghats

Share this article
WhatsApp Twitter Facebook
Link copied!
Published on: 27 Jul 2025

Tucked away within the misty hills of Valparai, Birla Waterfalls (also known as Birala Waterfalls) is one of Tamil Nadu’s lesser-known yet visually striking natural retreats. Surrounded by emerald-green tea estates, thick forests, and the tranquil rhythm of hill country life, this charming waterfall provides a welcome escape for those seeking something beyond the typical tourist trail.

Though modest in scale compared to more popular falls, Birla Waterfalls offers serenity, clean mountain air, and an authentic slice of Valparai's eco-rich landscape. It’s a perfect stop for nature lovers, photographers, and weekend travelers looking to experience the untouched beauty of the Anamalai hills.

Where is Birla Waterfalls?

Birla Waterfalls is located just 2.5 kilometers from Valparai Bus Stand, inside the Sholayar Tea Estate — which is operated by Jayshree Tea and Industries, part of the Birla Group. The estate is a working tea plantation, and the waterfall lies along the Nadumalai River, which originates from the upper reaches of the Grass Hills.

This scenic spot falls within private property, though it has been opened to visitors with basic entry controls and nominal charges.

Routes & How to Reach

From Coimbatore:

  • Distance: ~105 km
  • Route: Coimbatore → Pollachi → Aliyar Dam → Valparai (via SH-78)
  • Travel Time: ~3.5 to 4.5 hours

From Pollachi:

  • Distance: ~65 km
  • Highlights: Scenic ghat with 40+ hairpin bends, views of Aliyar Reservoir, Monkey Falls, and Loam’s Viewpoint.

From Kochi / Kerala:

  • Route: Chalakudy → Athirapally → Vazhachal → Malakkappara → Valparai
  • Note: This route is seasonal and may close during monsoon or forest restrictions.

Local Transport in Valparai

  • Auto Rickshaws: Available near bus stand (~₹150–₹200 round-trip)
  • Shared Jeeps: Hireable from hotels
  • Walkable: If staying nearby, it’s a ~20 min walk through estate paths

Public buses do not go directly to the waterfall.

Entry Details & Restrictions

FeatureDetails
Entry Fee₹15 per person
Timings8:00 AM to 5:00 PM
Swimming/BathingNot allowed
FacilitiesNo toilets, basic parking
AccessThrough estate road (light trek)

Best Time to Visit

  • October to February: Ideal for pleasant weather and manageable water flow
  • June to September: Avoid peak monsoon; slippery, muddy paths and water surges
  • March to May: Moderate flow, sunny weather

Water Flow & Rainy Season Alert

  • Flow peaks during and just after monsoon
  • Avoid visiting during or immediately after heavy rains due to safety concerns
  • Forest or estate staff may temporarily restrict entry

What to Expect

  • Multi-tiered cascade over rock ledges
  • Surrounded by lush tea bushes and shady trees
  • Perfect for quiet nature walks and photography
  • Total visit time: ~1 to 2 hours

Photography Tips

  • Morning light is best
  • Mist and fog add dramatic depth
  • Use a polarizer to reduce water glare

Safety & Responsible Travel

  • Wear sturdy footwear
  • No loud music or drones
  • Don’t pluck plants or trespass beyond trails
  • Carry drinking water and light snacks
  • Dispose of waste responsibly

Nearby Attractions

  • Nallamudi Viewpoint – Panoramic hills and valleys
  • Sholayar Dam – Large reservoir surrounded by forest
  • Monkey Falls – Popular roadside cascade
  • Chinnakallar Falls – Wettest zone in South India
  • Balaji Temple – Inside a private tea estate

In Summary

Birla Waterfalls is one of Valparai’s best-kept secrets — easy to reach, refreshingly peaceful, and wrapped in natural beauty. While it doesn’t boast roaring torrents or high-altitude thrills, it delivers what many travelers now crave: quiet, clean nature and a chance to breathe freely.

Whether you’re on a family outing, a solo photography trail, or a romantic hill stay, this gentle cascade surrounded by emerald tea hills will leave a lasting impression.

Just remember — take nothing but photos, leave nothing but footprints.

Quick Info

Where is Birla Waterfalls located?
Birla Waterfalls is situated about 2.5 kilometers from Valparai Bus Stand inside the Sholayar Tea Estate, which is managed by Jayshree Tea and Industries, a part of the Birla Group.
What is the Nadumalai River mentioned in the article?
The Nadumalai River is the water source for Birla Waterfalls and originates from the upper reaches of the Grass Hills in the Anamalai hills region.
How do I get to Birla Waterfalls from Coimbatore?
From Coimbatore, you can reach Birla Waterfalls by traveling approximately 105 km via Pollachi, Aliyar Dam, and Valparai along State Highway 78, taking around 3.5 to 4.5 hours.
Is the route from Kochi to Valparai open all year?
The route from Kochi through Chalakudy, Athirapally, Vazhachal, and Malakkappara to Valparai is seasonal and may be closed during the monsoon or due to forest restrictions.
What is the best time to visit Birla Waterfalls?
The ideal time to visit is from October to February for pleasant weather and manageable water flow. It's best to avoid the peak monsoon months due to slippery paths and heavy rain.
Is there an entry fee to visit Birla Waterfalls?
Yes, visitors are required to pay an entry fee of ₹15 per person to access the waterfall.
Are visitors allowed to swim or bathe at Birla Waterfalls?
No, swimming or bathing is not allowed at Birla Waterfalls due to safety and conservation measures.
What are the visiting hours for Birla Waterfalls?
Birla Waterfalls is open to visitors from 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM daily.
How can visitors reach Birla Waterfalls from Valparai town?
Visitors can reach Birla Waterfalls from Valparai Bus Stand by walking for about 20 minutes through estate paths, or by hiring auto-rickshaws or shared jeeps.
Are public buses available to Birla Waterfalls?
No, public buses do not go directly to the waterfall. Visitors must use local transportation or walk.
What are the facilities available at Birla Waterfalls?
The site offers basic amenities, including a light trekking path and limited parking, but no toilet facilities.
What should visitors bring when going to Birla Waterfalls?
Visitors should wear sturdy shoes, carry drinking water and light snacks, and be prepared to manage without restrooms or food stalls nearby.
What kind of footwear is recommended for visiting the waterfall?
Sturdy, non-slip footwear is recommended due to uneven, potentially muddy terrain, especially during or after rain.
How long does it take to visit Birla Waterfalls?
The total visit typically takes between 1 to 2 hours, including walking, enjoying the view, and taking photographs.
What makes Birla Waterfalls unique compared to other waterfalls?
Birla Waterfalls is less crowded and surrounded by scenic tea estates, offering a tranquil experience ideal for nature lovers and photographers.

In-Depth Answers

Is photography allowed at Birla Waterfalls?
Yes, photography is allowed and even encouraged, especially in the morning when lighting is ideal and mist adds a dramatic effect.
Can drones be used at Birla Waterfalls?
No, the use of drones is prohibited to maintain peace and prevent disturbance to the natural environment.
Are there any restrictions during the rainy season?
Yes, during or immediately after heavy rainfall, entry may be temporarily restricted by estate or forest staff due to safety concerns like slippery paths and water surges.
What nearby attractions can be visited along with Birla Waterfalls?
Nearby attractions include Nallamudi Viewpoint, Sholayar Dam, Monkey Falls, Chinnakallar Falls, and Balaji Temple.
Who operates the estate where Birla Waterfalls is located?
The Sholayar Tea Estate, where the waterfall is located, is operated by Jayshree Tea and Industries, under the Birla Group.
Is Birla Waterfalls suitable for families with children?
Yes, it is suitable for families, though visitors should supervise children closely and be cautious due to the natural terrain and lack of safety barriers.
Are there any food stalls or restaurants near the waterfall?
No, there are no food stalls or restaurants near the waterfall, so visitors should bring their own refreshments.
What kind of natural scenery surrounds Birla Waterfalls?
The waterfall is surrounded by lush tea bushes, shady trees, and rolling hill landscapes, creating a serene and picturesque environment.
What travel advice is given for visiting Birla Waterfalls responsibly?
Visitors are advised to avoid loud music, not pluck plants, stay on marked trails, carry out their trash, and respect the natural surroundings.
Can the waterfall be accessed by elderly visitors or those with mobility issues?
Access involves a light trek on estate paths, which may be manageable for some elderly visitors but could be challenging for those with mobility issues.
Is the waterfall visible all year round?
Yes, the waterfall generally flows year-round, but its intensity varies, peaking during and just after the monsoon season.
What kind of waterfall is Birla Waterfalls?
Birla Waterfalls is a multi-tiered cascade that flows gently over rock ledges, offering a scenic and peaceful ambiance rather than a powerful torrent.
How much does an auto-rickshaw round trip cost from Valparai to Birla Waterfalls?
A round-trip auto-rickshaw ride from Valparai to the falls typically costs between ₹150 and ₹200.
Is there any historical or cultural significance to Birla Waterfalls?
The article does not mention any specific historical or cultural significance, but the name and location link it to the Birla Group’s tea estates.
What is the general travel mood or experience offered by Birla Waterfalls?
The waterfall offers a peaceful, off-the-beaten-path experience ideal for travelers seeking tranquility, nature, and a slower pace away from crowded tourist spots.
