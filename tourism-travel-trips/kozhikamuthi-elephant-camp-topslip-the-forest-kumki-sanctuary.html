Kozhikamuthi Elephant Camp, Topslip: The Forest Kumki Sanctuary

Share this article

Published on: 27 Jul 2025

Deep within the Anamalai Tiger Reserve (ATR) near Topslip, Kozhikamuthi Elephant Camp serves as a vital hub for trained kumki elephants, tribal mahouts, and forest wildlife operations. Managed by local Malasar tribes under the Tamil Nadu Forest Department, the camp blends wildlife conservation, tribal heritage, and responsible tourism.

Why Visit Kozhikamuthi Camp?

  • Meet trained kumki elephants used in rescue, conflict mitigation, and forest patrols.
  • Experience authentic tribal elephant knowledge handed down through generations of Malasar mahouts.
  • Witness elephant routines like bathing, feeding, and daily interaction according to seasonal schedules.
  • Recognized for its standards: the camp received ISO 9001:2015 certification for safety and hygiene in 2023.

Location & Setting

  • Located in the Ulanthy Range, within the Anamalai Tiger Reserve, Tamil Nadu.
  • Approximately 30 km from Pollachi and 45 minutes‚Äô drive from Topslip eco-tourism lodge.
  • Surrounded by dense Western Ghats forest, tribal settlements, and wildlife corridors.

Getting There

By Own Vehicle

  1. From Pollachi, drive to Topslip via Aliyar Check Post.
  2. Continue on the forest drive for ~30 minutes; road is narrow‚Äîuse eco-tour vehicles or jeep.
  3. Kozhikamuthi Camp is ~6‚Äì8 km from Topslip internal road.

By Public Transport

  • Public buses connect Pollachi with Topslip/Sethumadai.
  • Near Topslip, hire an auto or shared jeep operated as part of forest tourist packages.

Entry & Booking Requirements

  • Kozhikamuthi Camp is located within the core zone of ATR, hence entry is restricted and regulated.
  • Visitors must go through Topslip eco-tour or forest department booking for permits.
  • Booking includes:
    • Forest entry pass
    • Jeep safari
    • Mahout-led elephant interaction
    • Guides and ranger fees

Contact Anamalai Tiger Reserve‚ÄîPollachi Division for permits. Tourism offices at Topslip also facilitate bookings.

Elephant Camp Experience

  • Visitors can observe morning routines: bathing, feeding with ragi-based dough, supervised by mahouts.
  • Camp houses about 26 trained elephants, including celebrated kumkis such as Kaleem.
  • Guests can take photos with elephants, learn about their diets, training, and the camp's role in rescue and forest work.
  • Elephants roam forest areas during day and return in evening for fodder feeding.

Wildlife & Forest Drives

  • The approach route and surrounding forests are rich in wildlife:
    • Peacocks, wild boar, deer, Nilgiri langur, and lion-tailed macaques are commonly seen.
  • Forest drives through ATR‚Äôs Ulanthy range often yield bird sightings like hornbills, Malabar barbets, and kingfishers.

Best Time to Visit

  • October to March: Best weather for elephants and wildlife viewing.
  • April‚ÄìMay: Some elephants are shifted to wetter zones like Varagaliyar or Chinnar due to water scarcity.
  • June‚ÄìSeptember: Monsoon; access may be restricted, roads slippery, caution advised.

Facilities & Visitor Guidelines

  • Facilities are basic: information board, safety instructions signages, toilets being added as proposed in 2023.
  • Visitors can interact or photograph elephants, but must:
    • Follow guide and mahout instructions
    • Avoid feeding or touching animals without supervision
    • Stay in designated zones only

Where to Stay

  • Topslip Forest Lodge or Eco-Cottages: Basic forest accommodation managed by Tamil Nadu Tourism and Forest Department.
  • Private Homestays in Sethumadai: Riverside resorts and cottages offer more comfort and local experiences.
  • In Pollachi, hotels like Sakthi, Ambrra, or Coconut County provide upgraded stays with easy access.

Wildlife Notes & Recent Events

  • A one-month-old orphaned wild elephant calf was relocated to Kozhikamuthi camp in May 2025 after rescue attempts failed.
  • Due to drought in April 2024, many captive elephants were relocated temporarily to Varagaliyar, Chinnar, and Manambolly ranges for better water and fodder access.

  • Maintain cleanliness‚Äîno plastic, use biodegradable means.

  • Follow all safety briefings and exit instructions in case of emergencies.
  • Keep your visit short and avoid crowding the camp area.

Summary

Kozhikamuthi Elephant Camp is not a zoo-style attraction; it is a real-world forest elephant workshop, steeped in tribal tradition, forest safety, and conservation. A visit here means witnessing kumki elephants in their daily routines, learning mahout culture, exploring rugged forest drives, and participating in a sensitive eco-tourism model rooted in ATR‚Äôs conservation goals.

If you‚Äôre planning a trip through Pollachi‚ÄìTopslip‚ÄìAnamalai, carve out a morning for Kozhikamuthi‚Äîit‚Äôs not just a stop, but a silent, soulful lesson on tamed giants and their forest legacy.

Quick Info

Where is Kozhikamuthi Elephant Camp located?
Kozhikamuthi Elephant Camp is located in the Ulanthy Range of the Anamalai Tiger Reserve in Tamil Nadu, about 30 km from Pollachi and approximately a 45-minute drive from Topslip eco-tourism lodge.
What makes Kozhikamuthi Elephant Camp unique?
The camp is managed by the Malasar tribal community and houses trained kumki elephants used in forest patrols and rescue operations. It blends tribal knowledge, conservation, and responsible tourism.
How can I reach Kozhikamuthi Elephant Camp from Pollachi?
Drive from Pollachi to Topslip via Aliyar Check Post, then continue on a narrow forest road for about 30 minutes. The camp is around 6‚Äì8 km from the Topslip internal road.
Can I use public transport to reach the camp?
Public buses run to Topslip or Sethumadai from Pollachi. From there, visitors must hire an auto or shared jeep to reach the camp as part of forest tourism packages.
Is prior booking required to visit the camp?
Yes, since the camp is in the core zone of the reserve, visitors need permits. Bookings must be made through the Topslip eco-tour office or the Anamalai Tiger Reserve ‚Äì Pollachi Division.
What does the Kozhikamuthi camp experience include?
Visitors can observe elephant routines such as bathing and feeding, interact with mahouts, learn about elephant training, and view forest patrol elephants like Kaleem.
How many elephants live at Kozhikamuthi Camp?
The camp houses around 26 trained elephants, including several celebrated kumkis used for forest operations and wildlife conflict resolution.
Can visitors touch or feed the elephants?
No, touching or feeding elephants is not allowed unless supervised by mahouts. Visitors must stay in designated zones and follow guide instructions.
Is photography allowed at the camp?
Yes, photography is permitted, especially during scheduled elephant routines. Visitors should avoid flash and loud sounds to not disturb the animals.
When is the best time to visit Kozhikamuthi Elephant Camp?
The best time is from October to March, with cooler weather and better chances for wildlife sightings. Avoid monsoon months (June‚ÄìSeptember) due to access challenges.
Are any facilities available for visitors at the camp?
Facilities are basic. Signage and safety boards are present, and toilets were proposed in 2023. Visitors should bring their own essentials and avoid littering.
Where can I stay near the elephant camp?
Accommodation options include forest lodges and eco-cottages at Topslip, private riverside stays in Sethumadai, and hotels in Pollachi like Sakthi, Ambrra, or Coconut County.
What wildlife might I see around the camp?
Wildlife sightings include peacocks, wild boars, Nilgiri langurs, deer, lion-tailed macaques, and birds like hornbills, kingfishers, and Malabar barbets.
Has the camp received any recognition or certification?
Yes, in 2023, the Kozhikamuthi Elephant Camp received ISO 9001:2015 certification for its hygiene and safety standards.
What role do the Malasar tribe play at the camp?
Malasar tribal mahouts manage the elephants, using generational knowledge to care for, train, and guide them in forest conservation work.

In-Depth Answers

What happened to the orphaned elephant calf rescued in 2025?
A one-month-old wild elephant calf was relocated to Kozhikamuthi in May 2025 after failed reintegration attempts, to be cared for and eventually trained.
What happens to the elephants during the dry season?
During droughts, such as in April 2024, many elephants are relocated temporarily to wetter forest zones like Varagaliyar, Chinnar, or Manambolly for better resources.
Can I include Kozhikamuthi in an eco-tourism package?
Yes, most Topslip eco-tour packages include a visit to Kozhikamuthi as part of their jeep safari and forest experience itinerary.
Is the camp suitable for children and families?
Yes, the camp offers a safe, educational experience ideal for families. However, children should be supervised at all times around the elephants.
Are any other elephant camps nearby?
Yes, Varagaliyar Elephant Camp is about 15 km away and offers a similar but more intimate elephant conservation experience.
What precautions should I take when visiting the camp?
Avoid loud noises, respect the tribal staff, don't litter, follow guide instructions, and stay within designated visitor areas.
Is the camp a commercial tourist attraction?
No, it is a functioning conservation and training camp focused on forest operations and eco-tourism, not a zoo or entertainment park.
Are there any organized forest drives available from the camp?
Yes, forest drives through the Ulanthy Range are part of eco-tour packages and offer chances to see wildlife and explore the Anamalai landscape.
How long does a typical visit to Kozhikamuthi take?
A typical visit lasts about 1‚Äì2 hours, depending on the schedule of elephant routines and the tour itinerary from Topslip.
Can I book online for visiting the elephant camp?
Currently, most bookings must be made in person or through forest offices at Topslip or Pollachi. Online systems are limited or unavailable.
Is the camp accessible for seniors or those with mobility issues?
Access involves forest terrain and limited infrastructure, so mobility-challenged visitors should inquire in advance about assistance or alternate arrangements.
What should I wear for a visit to the elephant camp?
Wear muted colors, comfortable walking shoes, and avoid perfumes or flashy clothing to minimize disturbance to animals and blend with the forest environment.
Can I visit the camp during monsoon?
Visits are possible but not recommended during heavy monsoon months (June‚ÄìSeptember) due to slippery roads and possible access restrictions.
What conservation goals does the camp support?
The camp supports elephant rescue, training, forest patrolling, tribal employment, and sustainable eco-tourism aligned with Anamalai Tiger Reserve goals.
