Non-Interlocked Manned Level Crossings in Indian Railways: A Persistent Safety Challenge

Indian Railways isn’t just a transport system—it’s a living, breathing lifeline that connects the aspirations, chaos, and contradictions of over a billion people. At RagaDecode, we decode this colossal network not with nostalgia, but with nuance.

Our coverage spans it all: from major accidents that expose gaps in safety systems to the gleaming launch of high-speed Vande Bharat trains that promise a new era of rail travel. We examine the stories behind derailments, fire hazards, delayed upgrades, and recurring lapses in maintenance—and ask who’s accountable.

We break down the reform agenda: privatization proposals, dynamic pricing controversies, platform redevelopment projects, and the digital shift toward smart ticketing. Whether it’s the struggle of migrant workers on unreserved compartments or the luxury of executive coaches, we tell the human side of railway policy.

Through real-world stories, we explore how small towns depend on slow trains for livelihood and mobility, while metros race ahead with dedicated freight corridors and metro rail extensions. We analyze why freight earnings still outpace passenger revenue and what that means for public subsidy and budget allocations.

We also dive into labor issues—driver fatigue, contractual workers, and the fading glory of the railway job. And when strikes halt operations or budget cuts delay projects, we simplify the impact for citizens who rely on the train as their only affordable commute.

Every viral clip—from overcrowded local trains in Mumbai to heartwarming gestures by railway staff—gets decoded with empathy and context. We look at the unsung heroes who clean the bogies, maintain signals, or help stranded passengers—all while the system grapples with modernization.

Our decoded reports also bring attention to overlooked regions: gauge conversions in the northeast, mountain railway preservation in Nilgiris, and tourism pushes on heritage routes. Whether it’s Tatkal booking glitches or budget allocation breakdowns, we keep it simple, sharp, and rooted in real India.

At RagaDecode, we don’t just report railway news—we make it understandable. Because when the train stops, India notices. And when it runs, it carries more than passengers—it carries the pulse of a nation.
