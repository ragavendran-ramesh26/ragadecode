Beyond the battlefield lies a colder war — of propaganda, pipeline power, media suppression, and legacy restoration. RagaDecode follows Russia’s maneuvers, front and back.

Ukraine Endures Deadliest Aerial Onslaught in Years as Russia Unleashes Over 500 Strikes
Ragavendran Ramesh • News Articles • Published on 4 Jul 2025

Russia launches its largest aerial attack on Ukraine in 4 years, deploying 537 drones & missiles. A Ukrainian F-16 pilot dies heroically after downing 7 targets. Read the latest on escalating warfare, civilian toll, and global response.

Russia’s news is no longer just about what happens — it’s about what’s allowed to be said. As the Kremlin tightens control over internal dissent and fuels external disruption, Russia projects a powerful image of nationalism, military might, and energy dominance. The ongoing war in Ukraine overshadows much of the international coverage, but internally, Russia grapples with an economy under layered sanctions, growing conscription fatigue, brain drain among its youth, and a crackdown on NGOs, artists, and opposition leaders. Cities like Moscow and St. Petersburg remain operational and outwardly resilient, but beyond them, regions face inflation, propaganda, and quiet protest. Russian state media pushes narratives of sovereignty and Western hypocrisy, while encrypted platforms host a growing network of activists, draft evaders, and war objectors. Putin’s grip is absolute but constantly watched — with dissent criminalized and memorials replaced with surveillance. Energy exports to China, BRICS realignment, Arctic expansion, and Africa military diplomacy reflect a long game that’s both reactive and strategic. RagaDecode traces Russia’s maneuvers not just through the missiles, but through its manipulated media and silenced streets.
