Iran, ruled by clerical authority yet constantly challenged by its own population, is a nation where headlines often mask the weight of silent resistance and strategic maneuvering. Under international sanctions and domestic surveillance, Iranians balance inflation, isolation, and increasingly digital dissent. News from Iran ranges from women-led hijab protests and anti-regime graffiti to drone deployments, nuclear deal speculation, and proxy wars in Syria, Iraq, and Yemen. The morality police, once subdued, reemerged as agents of control, but were met with viral defiance in cities like Tehran, Mashhad, and Isfahan. The regime maintains a grip via Revolutionary Guards, cyber crackdowns, and foreign policy opacity, but generations of youth increasingly push for freedoms — from dance videos on Instagram to encrypted organizing apps. Natural disasters like earthquakes, air pollution in Ahvaz, and train derailments are also underreported amid censorship layers.
